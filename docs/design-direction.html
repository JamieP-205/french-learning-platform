Design direction for sign-off

The learning garden, drawn by hand.

One idea holds the whole app together: your French grows like the walled garden in the progress painting. Warm limestone paper, that slate blue door, olive greens and dusty terracotta blooms. Daytime garden in light mode, the same garden by lantern light in dark mode. Nothing neon, nothing corporate, everything looking like it was chosen by a person.

1. Palette, daylight

Every colour is sampled from or tuned to the garden painting. The current app colours survive as warmer, softer versions of themselves, and the door finally gets its say: slate blue becomes the secondary colour for links and quiet structure.

5. Illustration style

The rule for every drawn thing in the app: it must look like it belongs inside the garden painting. Layered translucent shapes, sampled colours, soft irregular edges. No flat circles, no perfect geometry, no clip art.

Garden pieces today

Solid fills and perfect circles. Reads as stickers pasted onto a painting.

Garden pieces proposed

Gradient washes, irregular petal silhouettes, a whisper of texture. Sits inside the painting instead of on top of it.

Remy, redrawn

Same warm palette, soft edges, and a slate scarf borrowed from the garden door. Poses (waving, thinking, celebrating) are class-switched SVG groups; idle life is a blink and a slow tail sway in CSS, still under reduced motion.

Principles

  • Colours come from the painting, never from a default palette.
  • Gradient washes and turbulence give a watercolour edge at under 2 KB per piece.
  • Decorative SVG stays aria-hidden; text always carries the meaning.
  • Every animation has a still version for reduced motion.
  • Raster images only where paint genuinely beats vectors (the garden background stays).

7. Navigation and homepage, the one-page map

Homepage. A real front door: the garden world above the fold, one clear start button, an honest three-step explanation of teach before test, a peek at the first lesson, and a short honest line about what the course covers today. Signed-in visitors get taken straight back to their day.

Top navigation (desktop). Today, Learn, Review, Progress, Friends, Tutor, Settings, unchanged in order, restyled. The wordmark gains a small fox mark and goes to Today when signed in.

Mobile bar. Five labelled icon tabs: Today, Learn, Review, Progress, More. Review gets promoted because spaced review is the heart of the product. The More menu closes on outside taps and returns focus properly.

Lesson screen. Gains a minimal header: the wordmark plus Save and exit, so nobody is ever stranded mid-lesson without navigation. Remy joins the lesson later, in the garden-and-mascot batch.

Footer. Privacy, Terms, Service status, Try a lesson, unchanged.

8. What happens after sign-off

  • Colours become CSS variables with today’s values first, a pixel-identical refactor.
  • Dark mode arrives behind a header sun and moon toggle plus a settings control, defaulting to your device, saved per account.
  • The refreshed palette, brand marks (favicon, social image), and the new navigation land page by page.
  • The copy pass rewrites every corporate line in a warm human voice and a release-gate check keeps em dashes out for good.

Everything on this page is self-contained: no webfonts, no external images, no scripts. The contrast numbers were computed, not eyeballed, and the checking script ships with the repo.
